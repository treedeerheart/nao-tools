Band 1 — Builders
Core contributors · Module collaborators · Package builders
Interests
Elegant p2p architecture, local-first tech, sovereignty as a first principle, novel technical problems, peer recognition in builder communities
Where they are now
Scattered across Holochain, open source, crypto/web3 — already frustrated with centralized tooling, reachable through NAO's extended network
Channels
GitHub, Signal, direct personal invitation, developer-focused blog, async technical docs
Journey
Awareness of NAOMS → curiosity about architecture → hands-on access (package or module) → ongoing contributor with stake in the vision
Band 2 — Network
NAO members · Peer orgs: Holochain, Coasis, Hypha
Interests
Interoperability, shared values, tools that strengthen community fabric, not reinventing the wheel, being part of something larger without losing their identity
Where they are now
NAO members on Signal/Obsidian — waiting for something better. Peer orgs aware of similar problems, open if trust is established through shared contacts
Channels
Existing NAO Signal comms, gatherings and events, org-to-org outreach, vision/philosophy blog, word of mouth through trusted contacts
Journey
Recognition that NAOMS solves a real problem → invitation to explore → active use in their context → co-evolution and feedback loop with the project
Band 3 — Extended Community
Friends & family of members · Investors · External roles
Interests
Connection through someone they trust inside NAO, simplicity of use, feeling included without needing to understand the tech; investors: social impact with real return
Where they are now
Completely outside — no context for NAOMS. Relationship is entirely through a NAO member or the project's media and public presence
Channels
Personal invitation only. For investors: structured pitch moments, events, the TV show as a front door
Journey
Trusted person invites them → frictionless onboarding → light ongoing use (sharing, connecting) → optional deeper engagement if appetite grows
